## Scores

### Contextual Moat — 4/5 
*Waymo is deeply connected to the full transportation workflow: finding a ride, planning a route, navigating traffic, following safety rules, and giving passengers a reliable experience.*

**Score rationale:**
Waymo has a strong moat because self-driving cars need deep knowledge of roads, safety rules, passengers, and real-time traffic situations.
**Named attacker (from partner challenge):**
Tesla could be a strong attacker because it already has electric cars, AI driving technology, a large customer base, and strong brand trust.
Uber
Uber is a strong attacker because it already owns the ride-hailing customer relationship. People are used to opening Uber when they need transportation, so if Uber partners with autonomous vehicle companies, it could become a serious threat to Waymo.

---

### Data Advantage — 5/5
*Waymo sees real-world driving behavior: traffic patterns, pedestrian movements, road conditions, difficult intersections, and unexpected events. This data is specific to autonomous driving and becomes more valuable as more people use Waymo.*

**Score rationale:**
Waymo’s biggest advantage is data. Every real and simulated ride helps its AI learn more about driving, safety, and unusual road situations.

**Named attacker (from partner challenge):**
Tesla could challenge Waymo because it also collects large amounts of real-world driving data from its vehicles.
However, Waymo’s advantage is that its data is highly focused on autonomous ride-hailing, safety, complex city driving, and fully driverless passenger experiences.
---

### Platform Exposure — 3/5
*Waymo has medium platform exposure because large technology companies could build autonomous driving features into their own platforms, cars, or mobility services. If a major company ships a similar self-driving feature, Waymo still has an advantage because it already has real-world driverless experience, safety data, city partnerships, and a working robotaxi service.*

**Score rationale:**
The risk is real because big companies have strong AI, hardware, platforms, and user reach. However, Waymo’s product is not easy to copy quickly because autonomous driving requires testing, regulation, safety approval, and trust.
**Named attacker (from partner challenge):**
Uber, Tesla, and Zoox
Uber could threaten Waymo through its ride-hailing platform and customer base. Tesla could threaten Waymo through its car network and autonomous driving technology. Zoox could also be a strong attacker because it is focused on autonomous robotaxi services.
---

## Top Vulnerability
<Waymo’s biggest strategic risk is that a major competitor like Tesla could scale faster by using its large car network and real-world driving data.>

## Confidence Level
<I am moderately confident in this bet. Waymo has strong technology, safety experience, and data, but the autonomous driving market is still changing quickly, and competition from Tesla and other big tech companies is a serious risk.>
